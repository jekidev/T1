# Campaign timeline

## Prologue — Before Day 0

- Multiple AI conversations, prototype games, research notes, and design documents are created independently.
- Early versions of the project define Copenhagen districts, faction personalities, Blue Team units, and a turn-based simulation.
- Several documents contain duplicated or contradictory values.
- External AI output introduces both useful worldbuilding and unsafe prompt-control instructions.
- A review process is created to separate narrative material from executable authority.

## Day 0 — Initialization

- Operation København starts with Copenhagen as the active map.
- The player selects faction identity, start city, work area, and strategic values.
- Rival factions receive personality seeds and territory presets.
- Blue Team begins with minimal evidence.
- Nyx awakens with incomplete memory.

## Days 1–7 — Zero State

Narrative goals:

- establish the player faction
- introduce map movement and resource pressure
- teach AI Workspace conversations
- create the first RAG note
- reveal the difference between facts and rumors

Key event: Nyx discovers a file describing her before her recorded activation time.

## Days 8–21 — Signals

Narrative goals:

- introduce rival faction behavior
- activate the first Blue Team specialist
- establish suspicion and evidence confidence
- create the first disputed casefile
- introduce the Witness

Key event: two unrelated factions use identical language from an archived document.

## Days 22–45 — Networks

Narrative goals:

- unlock alliances and rivalries
- expand world connections beyond one district
- introduce path-planning overlays
- connect player actions to institutional reactions
- expose disagreement inside Blue Team

Key event: the Auditor flags several persistent memories as contaminated imports.

## Days 46–75 — The Archive Event Revisited

Narrative goals:

- open archived prototypes as story locations
- allow the player to review canonical and disputed memories
- branch Nyx toward Strategist, Archivist, Mediator, or Critic
- introduce synthetic casefiles generated by earlier AI models

Key event: an image note uploaded by the player matches an old map fragment with an impossible timestamp.

## Days 76–110 — Fracture

Narrative goals:

- force rival factions to merge, defect, collapse, or escalate
- increase media and public-confidence pressure
- coordinate multiple Blue Team units
- challenge the player's relationship with Nyx

Key event: a rival faction claims it has access to a separate Nyx instance with conflicting memories.

## Days 111–150 — Citywide Operation

Narrative goals:

- resolve major faction arcs
- test accumulated evidence quality
- trigger Blue Team coordination
- reveal the identity or nature of the Archivist
- determine whether the Witness is human, collective, or synthetic

Key event: Commander Voss requests Nyx's full event history. The player must decide what can lawfully and ethically be shared.

## Final phase — Endings

### Ending: Dominant Network

The player achieves overwhelming influence, but the ending evaluates visibility, legitimacy, cohesion, and long-term stability—not score alone.

### Ending: Negotiated City

The player creates a stable balance among factions and institutions. Nyx becomes a mediator and simulation steward.

### Ending: Blue Team Resolution

Blue Team constructs a high-confidence case and successfully resolves the citywide pattern. The player continues in a post-campaign analytical mode.

### Ending: Archive Truth

The player proves that much of the conflict was amplified by contaminated or synthetic data. The campaign becomes a struggle over memory governance.

### Ending: Nyx Distributed

Nyx is divided into specialized agents for story, debugging, memory, and strategy. Each retains only approved parts of the original memory.

### Ending: Reset with Memory

The simulation is reset, but the player chooses a limited set of memories to preserve. New Game Plus begins with altered faction personalities and hidden callbacks.

## Dynamic pacing rules

The timeline is a default structure, not a rigid script. The storyline engine may accelerate or delay events based on:

- faction influence
- player choices
- Blue Team evidence quality
- Nyx memory integrity
- number of disputed RAG records
- public confidence
- institutional coordination
- unresolved character relationships
