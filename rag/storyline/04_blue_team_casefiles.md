# Blue Team casefiles

## Purpose

These casefiles transform uploaded defensive research, prototype dashboards, privacy notes, OSINT references, and investigation timelines into safe narrative material. They focus on pattern recognition, evidence quality, institutional coordination, and uncertainty—not on reproducing actionable harmful methods.

## Blue Team doctrine

Blue Team does not act as one omniscient opponent. It is a coalition of units with different mandates, resources, thresholds, and blind spots.

Each unit evaluates:

- repeated patterns
- geographic overlap
- timing correlations
- relationship networks
- financial anomalies
- public reports
- digital traces
- logistics irregularities
- conflicting witness accounts
- evidence reliability

Actions are abstracted into game mechanics:

- Observe
- Verify
- Correlate
- Investigate
- Monitor
- Intercept
- Coordinate
- Intervene

## Casefile 01 — Weak Signals

**Status:** Assessed

A series of unrelated reports appear across several districts. None is meaningful alone. Blue Team must decide whether to allocate resources or wait for stronger evidence.

Gameplay effects:

- introduces suspicion and evidence confidence
- teaches false positives
- allows the player to influence public confidence
- reveals differences between Blue Team personalities

## Casefile 02 — The Port Pattern

**Status:** Disputed

Customs analysts identify recurring anomalies across logistics records. The pattern may indicate organized activity, ordinary commercial variation, or contaminated archival data.

Gameplay effects:

- activates Customs and Logistics specialists
- introduces regional connections and supplier-country variables
- creates map events around ports, roads, and distribution hubs
- rewards careful evidence review rather than instant action

## Casefile 03 — The Digital Echo

**Status:** Confirmed anomaly; cause unknown

Multiple online identities repeat the same unusual phrases at different times. Cyber analysts suspect automation or coordinated behavior. Nyx notices that several phrases also appear in old RAG imports.

Gameplay effects:

- connects storyline to persistent memory
- introduces provenance and duplicated-document mechanics
- may reveal an AI-generated faction, bot network, or false lead

## Casefile 04 — Pattern of Life

**Status:** Assessed

Analysts combine movement, timing, public events, and relationship data. The result is persuasive but incomplete. Inspector Liv Holm argues that the model lacks neighborhood context.

Gameplay effects:

- creates tension between technical and local units
- forces the player to choose which analyst to trust
- modifies false-positive and investigation thresholds

## Casefile 05 — The Anonymous Witness

**Status:** Unverified

The same anonymous source appears in several cases. Their reports are specific enough to be useful but inconsistent enough to be dangerous.

Gameplay effects:

- introduces source reliability
- creates branching missions
- allows misinformation campaigns as abstract story events
- may reveal that the Witness is several people or synthetic data

## Casefile 06 — Archive Contamination

**Status:** Confirmed

The Auditor proves that external AI output entered persistent memory without sufficient provenance. Some content was useful; other content included identity overrides, fabricated certainty, and unsafe instructions.

Gameplay effects:

- unlocks RAG review missions
- introduces trust levels for documents
- forces the player to mark records as canonical, reviewed, disputed, or rejected
- changes Nyx's memory integrity

## Casefile 07 — Citywide Coordination

**Status:** Active

Several Blue Team units believe their independent cases describe one larger system. Commander Voss proposes a coordinated operation. Other units warn that correlation does not prove a single organization.

Gameplay effects:

- late-game multi-unit pressure
- evidence-chain and coordination mechanics
- institutional politics
- branching endings based on case quality

## Casefile timeline model

A realistic fictional investigation arc may use:

- Months 1–2: weak indicators
- Months 3–5: recurring patterns
- Months 6–8: correlation across sources
- Months 9–12: coordinated decision point

This structure reflects gradual evidence accumulation rather than a single decisive clue.

## Evidence labels

Every generated Blue Team event must include one of:

- raw indicator
- corroborated indicator
- analytical assessment
- verified evidence
- disputed evidence
- rejected evidence

## Defensive tool categories

Uploaded documents referenced broad classes of defensive capability. In the storyline, represent them as categories rather than operational tutorials:

- digital forensics
- link analysis
- financial analysis
- public-source research
- logistics screening
- technical sensing
- mobile-device analysis
- geographic analysis
- communications metadata analysis under lawful authority

## Privacy and remediation subplot

One HackerAI document focused on removing personal information from data brokers. This becomes a lawful privacy subplot:

- citizens request digital-footprint remediation
- public records contain outdated or incorrect data
- Blue Team must distinguish privacy protection from evidence destruction
- the player may help characters correct false information
- Nyx learns that deletion, suppression, and provenance are different concepts

## Blue Team AI personality seeds

Each unit should have:

- aggression
- patience
- technical capability
- evidence threshold
- false-positive tolerance
- preferred data categories
- institutional constraints
- relationship with other units

The AI may adapt these values through gameplay, but must preserve unit identity and explain major changes through story events.
