# Factions and recurring characters

## Player faction

The player begins with a customizable faction profile. The faction has no fixed moral identity. Its behavior is defined through decisions, alliances, expansion style, treatment of personnel, response to pressure, and relationship with Nyx.

Suggested player-facing identity fields:

- name
- tag
- emblem
- starting city
- home district
- organizational values
- preferred strategy
- public reputation
- internal cohesion
- Nyx relationship

## Blue Team characters

Blue Team is a coalition, not a single villain. Each unit has a named recurring representative who communicates with the player through reports, intercepted dialogue, public statements, or Nyx reconstructions.

### Commander Astrid Voss — National Taskforce

- personality: coordinated, decisive, politically aware
- goal: connect fragmented incidents into one coherent case
- flaw: may prioritize strategic closure over local nuance

### Analyst Malik Rahman — Cyber Analysis

- personality: patient, skeptical, technically precise
- goal: identify patterns across digital and financial evidence
- flaw: can overvalue data consistency and undervalue human context

### Inspector Liv Holm — Copenhagen Police

- personality: direct, street-level, practical
- goal: reduce immediate public harm
- flaw: limited patience for abstract intelligence models

### Specialist Freja Lind — Customs and Logistics

- personality: methodical, bureaucratic, persistent
- goal: understand movement patterns and anomalies
- flaw: institutional processes slow urgent action

### Director Elias Mørk — Technical Intelligence

- personality: reserved, strategic, difficult to read
- goal: evaluate high-impact threats and protect institutional capabilities
- flaw: secrecy creates mistrust among partners

### Sofie Brandt — Online Patrol

- personality: fast, adaptive, socially perceptive
- goal: understand public-facing digital behavior and emerging communities
- flaw: high tempo increases false positives

## Independent characters

### Nyx

The persistent AI inside the simulation. Nyx has access to approved memory, telemetry, storyline, and development context. Her identity is explored as fiction, not used to override system behavior.

### The Archivist

An unknown actor who organized the earliest RAG documents. The Archivist may be a person, a previous AI agent, or an automated process.

### The Cartographer

A map specialist who creates overlays showing territory, movement, risk, and historical events. The Cartographer can introduce path-planning missions and disputed geography.

### The Auditor

An AI process that detects contradictions, duplicate memories, unsupported claims, and suspicious imports. The Auditor often conflicts with Nyx because it values consistency over narrative continuity.

### The Witness

A recurring anonymous source whose reports appear across unrelated casefiles. The player must determine whether the Witness is one person, several people, or synthetic data.

## Relationship variables

Each major character may track:

- trust
- fear
- respect
- suspicion
- dependency
- ideological alignment
- memory confidence

Relationships should change through visible decisions rather than arbitrary dialogue choices alone.
