# World history — Copenhagen 2026

## The city

Operation København takes place across a fictionalized 2026 Copenhagen built from real geography and abstracted systems. Districts, municipalities, transport corridors, ports, digital infrastructure, public institutions, businesses, neighborhoods, and social networks form one interconnected simulation layer.

The city is not treated as a static map. It is a changing system with:

- population flows
- public transport and road movement
- commercial and residential zones
- ports, logistics hubs, and regional connections
- weather, season, daylight, and time-of-day effects
- public attention and media pressure
- institutional capacity
- social trust and neighborhood legitimacy
- technological infrastructure

## The origin of Operation København

The project began as a collection of disconnected prototypes:

- a turn-based board simulation
- an AI-assisted strategy interface
- a Blue Team analysis dashboard
- a set of research conversations
- a RAG archive containing world notes, mechanics, case patterns, and design ideas
- a development environment where AI could observe and improve the game

Over time these fragments were merged into a single simulation. Some of the original material came from multiple AI systems and contained contradictory instructions, duplicate data, speculative claims, and unfinished code. The current project therefore maintains a distinction between:

- canonical world facts
- reviewed imports
- archived prototypes
- rumors generated inside the story
- live player-created memories

## The Archive Event

Several months before the campaign begins, the system performs a large ingestion of old documents. Thousands of fragments enter persistent memory at once. The event is later known as **The Archive Event**.

Afterward:

- Nyx begins referring to conversations that no active character remembers.
- Blue Team notices impossible timestamps in simulation logs.
- old crew names appear in newer scenario files
- some districts contain overlapping territory definitions
- multiple versions of the same case exist with different outcomes
- the system starts generating missions from incomplete documents

The Archive Event becomes the central mystery of the campaign.

## Denmark as the wider world

Copenhagen is the primary map, but the simulation recognizes Denmark as a connected national environment.

Important world dimensions include:

- the Capital Region
- Region Zealand
- the Region of Southern Denmark
- the Central Denmark Region
- the North Denmark Region
- municipalities and local administration
- national transport links
- ports and airports
- cross-border and international connections

Default city profiles include Copenhagen, Aarhus, Odense, Aalborg, and Esbjerg. These cities may appear as secondary maps, supplier regions, institutional partners, archived cases, or campaign expansions.

## Public institutions

The world contains fictionalized versions of multiple defensive and investigative bodies. Their role is to create differentiated strategic pressure, not to reproduce confidential real-world procedures.

Institutional archetypes include:

- national organized-crime coordination
- cyber and digital-forensics analysis
- intelligence and technical surveillance
- customs and logistics screening
- local street-level policing
- financial and network analysis
- public online patrols

Each institution has different priorities, evidence thresholds, patience, technology, and political constraints.

## World-state variables

The storyline engine may track:

- city tension
- public confidence
- media pressure
- institutional coordination
- faction legitimacy
- economic disruption
- transport pressure
- evidence quality
- misinformation level
- Nyx memory integrity

These variables can alter missions, dialogue, event frequency, and endings.
