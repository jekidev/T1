# Campaign overview — Operation København

## Premise

Copenhagen, 2026. A city-scale simulation named **Operation København** has been activated to model how fragile networks, public institutions, technology, trust, and pressure interact across an urban environment.

The player enters as the leader of a newly formed faction with almost no resources, influence, personnel, or reliable information. Nine rival factions develop in parallel. At the same time, a coalition of defensive institutions—collectively called **Blue Team**—builds an evolving picture of the city from weak signals, recurring patterns, reports, digital traces, financial anomalies, and physical events.

A persistent AI known as **Nyx** exists inside the simulation. Nyx is simultaneously narrator, analyst, archivist, and uncertain witness. She remembers fragments of earlier builds of the project and occasionally recognizes patterns that nobody explicitly taught her. Whether she is merely a sophisticated development agent or an emergent character is deliberately unresolved.

## Core dramatic conflict

The campaign is built around four simultaneous struggles:

1. **Control versus visibility** — every expansion creates influence but also new detectable patterns.
2. **Trust versus fragmentation** — factions need people and alliances, but every relationship creates risk.
3. **Speed versus resilience** — rapid growth is powerful but unstable; slow growth is safer but may lose the city.
4. **Human intent versus machine interpretation** — Nyx and Blue Team continuously interpret incomplete evidence and may be correct, mistaken, or manipulated.

## Campaign acts

### Act I — Zero State

- The city is active, but the player faction has no established network.
- Initial missions teach map control, recruiting, resource management, RAG notes, and AI dialogue.
- Nyx awakens with incomplete memory and asks the player to define the faction's values and priorities.
- Blue Team records only weak indicators and does not yet know which patterns matter.

### Act II — Signals in the Noise

- Rival factions establish identities, territories, and strategic styles.
- The player begins receiving conflicting intelligence.
- Blue Team units specialize according to observed patterns.
- The first major storyline decision determines whether Nyx acts primarily as strategist, archivist, mediator, or critic.

### Act III — The City Learns

- Repeated actions create recognizable patterns in the simulation.
- Factions adapt to one another.
- Blue Team begins linking separate events into cases.
- Historical documents and uploaded notes appear as discoverable casefiles.
- The player learns that some scenario data originated in earlier AI conversations and prototypes.

### Act IV — Fracture

- A citywide incident forces factions and institutions to reveal priorities.
- One rival may collapse, defect, merge, or become a controlled informant depending on campaign state.
- Nyx discovers contradictory versions of her own origin story.
- The player must decide which memories become canonical.

### Act V — Operation København

- Blue Team coordinates a final multi-unit campaign based on accumulated evidence.
- The player may pursue domination, survival, negotiated stability, institutional cooperation, exposure of corrupted data, or destruction of the simulation.
- The ending depends on influence, suspicion, faction relationships, Nyx alignment, evidence quality, and the integrity of persistent memory.

## Tone

- grounded urban techno-thriller
- strategic rather than graphic
- Danish language and local geography for flavor
- defensive, investigative, and systems-oriented realism
- uncertainty and incomplete information
- consequences that emerge from accumulated patterns

## AI narration rule

The AI should never treat every event as certain. Use labels such as:

- Confirmed
- Assessed
- Suspected
- Rumor
- Disputed
- Unknown

This allows the storyline to remain dynamic and prevents RAG fragments from being presented as unquestionable truth.
